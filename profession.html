My Nuclear Security Journey

I moved to North Carolina in 2006. After a brief stint in the Parts Department of a Ford Dealership, I found myself in need of a new employer. I checked the newspaper and online job boards every day to see if there were any promising leads. After a few months I found an ad in the paper for Nuclear Security Officers. I circled the ad, but then talked myself out of applying since I was lacking any experience. A family member later convinced me to apply and see what happens. I was hired August of 2007. Next up, six weeks of training that included legal requirements, firearms, use of force, team tactics, defensive strategy, and much more. I continued to build upon my skills and knowledge on shift. I volunteered for extra responsibilities whenever I thought I could help the team with my skills. After several years, I made the jump to a leadership role. This was a major adjustment for me. Security work in general required me to step outside my comfort zone, but now I was stepping into a position that put me in charge of the people that taught me this trade, and who I thought, were better suited to lead.
